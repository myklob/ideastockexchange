Interest Validity Debate Template

A validity question is just a claim. "This interest is valid," "this interest matters more than that one," and "in this standoff, safety outranks privacy" are all beliefs with reasons to agree and disagree, and they get scored the same way every other belief on the ISE does. This template is that pro/con skeleton applied to validity at three scopes.

When to use it: when the Interest Validity score is itself the fight. For the quick two-score read on an interest (Linkage Accuracy and Interest Validity together), use the Interest Evaluation Template and only expand to this page when validity is contested. This page is to the Validity score what a belief page is to a single argument: the place it gets its own tree.

For the definition and the two-score model, see Interest. For the criteria and the Maslow priors referenced below, see the Interest Scoring Methodology. This page applies that model, it does not restate it.

Scores below are illustrative placeholders. The live scoring engine is on the roadmap, not running yet.

The Interest and the Ground Rules

The interest: [state it as a need, fear, or desire, e.g. "I need to feel my neighborhood is safe"]

  • Default: treat the interest as valid until an argument lowers it. The burden is on the challenge.
  • Both sides are rational: assume people on each side hold their interest for reasons tied to a real need, not because they are stupid or evil.
  • Arguments set the score, nothing else: validity traces entirely to the scored reasons below. It is never set by who holds the interest, how powerful they are, or how loudly they assert it.
  • Maslow is a prior, not a verdict: where the need sits on the hierarchy is a starting point that the reasons below can move up or down.

Scope 3: Validity Within a Specific Conflict or Scenario

The contextual question, and the one that drives real conflict resolution. An interest that is generally valid can rank differently once a concrete scenario sets the facts.
