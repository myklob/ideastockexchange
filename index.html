Idea Stock Exchange

A prediction market for ideas, where arguments are scored, not just shouted.

The three pillars

ReasonRank

Each argument inherits strength from the arguments that support or undermine it — recursively. Strong claims earn their score; weak ones can’t hide behind volume.

Market Price

A belief’s share price reflects what the crowd thinks is true. Buy if you disagree with the price and can defend the position with evidence.

The arbitrage

When ReasonRank and Market Price diverge, there’s a trade. Logic-grounded conviction gets rewarded; popularity without reasoning does not.

Methodology

Beliefs are decomposed into reasons-to-agree and reasons-to-disagree. Each argument carries an impact score (how much it would matter if true) and a linkage score (how strongly it actually attaches to its parent claim). Sub-arguments propagate up: a reason is only as strong as the evidence and counter-evidence beneath it.

The result is a tree of claims with calibrated, transparent scores — not a karma counter. Two reasonable people who disagree on a conclusion can still agree on which sub-arguments are well-supported.

Applied examples

  • Public-policy debates with quantified cost-benefit ledgers.
  • Scientific claims paired with falsification criteria and evidence quality.
  • Product and design decisions, scored against measurable objective criteria.
  • Personal beliefs, examined the same way you’d examine anyone else’s.

The code

The application is a TypeScript / Next.js codebase with a Prisma-backed schema for beliefs, arguments, evidence, and linkage scores. Source lives at github.com/myklob/ideastockexchange.

The repository is the work-in-progress reference implementation. A hosted version of the application is a separate deployment.

Related projects

  • Prediction markets — price as aggregated probability.
  • Bayesian networks — propagation of evidence through structured claims.

Contribute

Issues, pull requests, and structured beliefs are all welcome. If you have a claim you’d like decomposed under this framework, open an issue on the repository describing the belief and its strongest opposing reasons.
