<p><span style="font-family: &quot;Segoe UI&quot;, &quot;Lucida Grande&quot;, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 20px; font-weight: bold;">Why Every Idea Deserves a Two-Column Debate Page</span></p>
<div>
<p><strong>Core Principle:</strong> Truth emerges from weighing the strongest evidence <em>for</em> a claim against the strongest evidence <em>against</em> it&mdash;simultaneously, not sequentially.</p>
<hr />
<h2>The Problem: Scattered Arguments Make Truth Invisible</h2>
<p>Right now, if you want to understand whether raising the minimum wage helps workers, you'll find:</p>
<ul>
<li>Pro-wage articles on progressive sites</li>
<li>Anti-wage articles on libertarian sites</li>
<li>Academic studies buried in paywalls</li>
<li>Partisan talking points on social media</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Result:</strong> You never see the best case for <em>and</em> against in one place. You choose your tribe, read their arguments, and think you've done your homework.</p>
<p>This is why debates never end. We're not actually engaging with the strongest version of the opposing view&mdash;we're fighting straw men while our opponents do the same.</p>
<hr />
<h2>The Solution: One Page Per Topic, Two Columns Per Page</h2>
<p>The Idea Stock Exchange treats every significant belief as deserving its own page where <em>all</em> perspectives converge and compete on merit. See: <a href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/w/page/159323433/One%20Page%20Per%20Topic">One Page Per Topic</a>.</p>
<p><strong>The Architecture:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Left Column: <a href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/Reasons">Reasons to Agree</a></strong><br /><strong>Right Column: Reasons to Disagree</strong></p>
<p>Not static lists. Live argument markets where <a href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/w/page/159353568/Evidence">Evidence</a> quality determines position.</p>
<hr />
<h2>How It Works: The ReasonRank Engine</h2>
<h3>1. Arguments Link to Evidence, Not Emotions</h3>
<p>Every reason to agree or disagree must connect to verifiable evidence. No evidence? The argument doesn't appear. See: <a href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/w/page/159353568/Evidence">Evidence Scores</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Tier System:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>T1:</strong> Peer-reviewed studies, official government data</li>
<li><strong>T2:</strong> Expert institutional analysis</li>
<li><strong>T3:</strong> Quality journalism, surveys</li>
<li><strong>T4:</strong> Opinion, anecdote</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Linkage Determines Strength</h3>
<p>An argument scores high only if it <em>actually supports the conclusion</em>. Logical fallacies (ad hominem, strawman, false dichotomy) get scored down automatically. See: <a href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/w/page/159338766/Linkage%20Scores">Linkage Scores</a>.</p>

If a study gets retracted, arguments citing it lose strength immediately. <h2 class="font-bold text-[1.125rem] -mb-1 mt-3 text-text-100">What This Enables</h2>
<h3 class="font-bold text-base -mb-1 mt-2 text-text-100">1. Nuance Becomes Accessible</h3>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">See the full landscape in five minutes instead of five hours of Google searches that lead to tribal echo chambers.</p>
<h3 class="font-bold text-base -mb-1 mt-2 text-text-100">2. Deception Becomes Difficult</h3>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">Weak arguments can't hide when placed next to strong evidence. Cherry-picked data gets exposed by the evidence tier system.</p>
<h3 class="font-bold text-base -mb-1 mt-2 text-text-100">3. Tribalism Becomes Obsolete</h3>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">Ideas judged on merit, not source. A strong conservative argument appears high in the right column <em>even if</em> you're progressive. You must engage with it.</p>
<h3 class="font-bold text-base -mb-1 mt-2 text-text-100">4. Expertise Becomes Transparent</h3>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">No more "trust the experts" vs "do your own research." The evidence trail is public. Anyone can verify. See: <a class="focus:decoration-current hover:decoration-current decoration-current/40 decoration-1 underline-offset-2 underline" href="https://myclob.pbworks.com/w/page/159353568/Evidence">Evidence Leaderboard</a>.</p>
<h3 class="font-bold text-base -mb-1 mt-2 text-text-100">5. Progress Becomes Measurable</h3>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">When new evidence appears, beliefs update automatically. We can track which predictions proved accurate. See: <a class="focus:decoration-current hover:decoration-current decoration-current/40 decoration-1 underline-offset-2 underline" href="#">Predictions &amp; Reality Check</a> in book analysis templates.</p>

<h2 class="font-bold text-[1.125rem] -mb-1 mt-3 text-text-100">The Meta-Principle: Process Over Positions</h2>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">Most organizations compete on <em>what</em> they believe. The Idea Stock Exchange competes on <em>how</em> we believe&mdash;the methodology itself.</p>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">We're not saying "Here's the right answer on minimum wage." We're saying "Here's how to evaluate competing claims about minimum wage systematically."</p>
<p class="leading-[1.7] whitespace-normal break-words font-claude-response-body">That's why two columns matter. They enforce intellectual honesty. You cannot claim to understand an issue until you can state the opposing view strongly enough that its advocates would agree with your characterization.</p>
