📖 Definitions and Scoring Concepts

Arguments vs. Evidence: Arguments are logical claims, scored by logical validity and linkage strength. Evidence is empirical data, scored by source tier and conclusion relevance. Argument Score = Evidence Quality x Logical Validity x Linkage Strength.

Truth / Importance / Linkage: Truth is the child belief's own net score. Importance weights how much the argument moves the needle (and can itself be sourced from a sub-belief). Linkage measures whether the reason actually connects to the conclusion. Impact = Truth x Importance x Linkage.

Evidence Tiers: T1 = peer-reviewed / official data. T2 = expert analysis / institutional reports. T3 = investigative journalism / surveys. T4 = opinion / anecdotal.

Spoiler effect: when a minor candidate draws votes from a similar major candidate, flipping the outcome.

Ballot exhaustion: a ballot stops counting once all its ranked candidates are eliminated.
