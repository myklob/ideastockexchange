Description

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to highly autonomous systems that outperform humans at most economically valuable work. This belief posits that such systems will be developed and operational by the year 2030, representing a transformative milestone in human technological development.

This prediction is based on several key observations: the rapid progress in transformer architectures and large language models, consistent scaling laws showing predictable improvements with increased compute and data, massive investment by major technology companies, and emergent capabilities appearing at scale.

The timeline assumes continued exponential growth in compute availability, no fundamental algorithmic blockers, and that current approaches (scaled appropriately) are sufficient to achieve general intelligence without requiring paradigm shifts in AI architecture.
